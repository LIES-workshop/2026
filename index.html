LIES 2026

Language Models, the Information Environment, and Society

Bridging language model research and social science to understand how AI is reshaping the information environment.

About

Large language models are now embedded in search engines, social media feeds, and news platforms—increasingly used by millions to navigate health topics, social issues, and political debates. Their societal consequences are significant: LLMs hold promise for democratizing access to information and lowering barriers to civic participation, but also can embed political biases, generate and amplify misinformation, and exhibit sycophantic behaviors that reinforce rather than inform.

Research on these effects, however, remains fragmented: NLP and machine learning communities focus on system development and benchmarking, while social scientists study downstream effects—and few researchers bridge both. This workshop uses the information environment as an integrating frame to foster the interdisciplinary community needed to study these systems rigorously and govern them responsibly.

Themes & Topics

The workshop invites contributions on, but not limited to, the following themes:

  • Political and ideological bias in LLMs Bias measurement, political opinion effects, and democratic participation.
  • LLMs and civic discourse Persuasion, deliberation, and democratic participation.
  • Misinformation LLMs in platforms, AI-generated misinformation, and synthetic actors.
  • Content moderation and AI agents Disclosure, content governance, and platform moderation.
  • Information-seeking, epistemic autonomy, and AI companions Over-reliance, sycophancy, health behavior, and fact-checking.
  • Evaluation and benchmarks Search/chatbot audits, benchmarks, and metrics.
  • Interventions and governance Policy approaches and technical interventions for mitigating societal harms.

Submissions

We welcome double-blind non-archival extended abstracts (two pages, excluding tables, figures, and references) via OpenReview using the COLM format, as well as invited talks. Submissions will be due June 16, 2026, with accept/reject notifications by July 24, 2026.

We are committed to supporting junior researchers and will actively recruit speakers and program committee members from underrepresented groups.
